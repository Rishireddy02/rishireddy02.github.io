Professional Bio

About

I am a cybersecurity practitioner-researcher with more than six years of experience in penetration testing, incident response, and malware analysis. My professional work spans web and API exploitation, OWASP Top 10 remediation, SIEM-based threat hunting, and adversary emulation aligned to the MITRE ATT&CK framework.

Over the last two years, my practice has shifted toward AI security — investigating adversarial attacks on machine learning models, prompt-injection vulnerabilities in large language models, and the new threat surface introduced when enterprises deploy generative AI in production. This shift is what brings me to AIML-501: Model Development and to the broader scholar-practitioner journey of building rigorous, defensible AI systems.

I hold a Master of Digital Forensics and Cyber Analysis from George Mason University and a Bachelor of Computer Science Engineering from Osmania University, and I am a Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH v10). My current research interests sit at the intersection of cybersecurity and applied machine learning — particularly model robustness, explainability, and the data-bias issues that shape downstream security outcomes.
